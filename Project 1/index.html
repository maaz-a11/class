Abdul Sattar Edhi

1928 - 2016

Angel of Mercy

Abdul Sattar Edhi (1 January 1928 – 8 July 2016) was a Pakistani philanthropist, ascetic, and humanitarian who founded the Edhi Foundation, which operates the largest volunteer ambulance service in the world. He was born in Bantva, British India (now in Gujarat, India), and moved to Karachi, Pakistan after partition in 1947. Known for his selfless dedication to the needy, Edhi established various social services, including orphanages, homeless shelters, rehabilitation centers, and medical facilities across Pakistan. Edhi was revered for his humble lifestyle and his mission to serve humanity, regardless of religion or race, earning him the title of "Angel of Mercy" in Pakistan.
